What is ECG?

  • ECG is a diagnostic test used to detect the electrical activity of the heart.

  • As electrical impulses pass thought the heart , the electrical spread current spread into the tissues surrounding the heart and and small portion of these currents spread into the surface of the body

  • These signals are sent by a group of cells in the right atrium of the heart known as the sinoatrial node (SA node): the primary pacemaker of the heart, and they spread through the heart muscle tissue as tiny electrical impulses.

  • An ECG measures these changes in electrical signals (or, in fact, voltage) on different areas of skin and plots them as a graph

  • Time Measurements and Heart Rate Measurements are made horizontally on electrocardiogram.
